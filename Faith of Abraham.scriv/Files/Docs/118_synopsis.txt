Just as he and Sarai had settled in their new home, severe famine struck the land. Abram was forced to choose: remain in the land of promise and risk death by starvation, or relocate temporarily to Egypt and risk death at the hand of foreign enemies. Abram had just received God’s promise that he would become a “great nation” (Gen 12:2), but that future now appeared to be in jeopardy.

Grigoni, M. R., Custis, M., Mangum, D., & Whitehead, M. M. (2012). Abraham: Following God’s Promise (Ge 12:10–20). Bellingham, WA: Lexham Press.
