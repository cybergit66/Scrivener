On another level, though, Abram’s actions can be understood as an attempt to preserve the future promised in Gen 12:2–3. In this light, Abram’s focus on his survival reflects a concern to neutralize threats against the fulfillment of God’s promise. How could a dead man father a great nation? This episode (and the parallel account of Gen 20) presents Abram struggling with fear—the fear of losing God’s promised inheritance
