Faith demands a ruthless abandonment of our past way of living (repentance), of the world system
Faith demands a ruthless abandonment of the past. Abraham has to leave the consolation of familiarity and tradition far behind. He has to jettison his family, his homeland, and the old ways of worship. But this abandonment leads to his fulfillment

Waltke, B. K., & Fredricks, C. J. (2001). Genesis: a commentary (p. 209). Grand Rapids, MI: Zondervan.
